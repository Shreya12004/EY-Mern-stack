Missal pav

Missal Pav (or Misal Pav) is a popular Maharashtrian street food dish enjoyed across India. It's a flavorful and spicy dish made with a lentil curry (called "Misal") and served with soft bread rolls (called "Pav"). Here's an overview:

Preparation

Misal Pav is a popular Maharashtrian dish made by serving spicy curry (misal) with soft bread rolls (pav). To prepare, cook sprouted moth beans (matki) with turmeric and salt until tender. For the curry, heat oil, add mustard seeds, curry leaves, chopped onions, and tomatoes, and saut√© with spices like red chili powder, garam masala, and goda masala. Mix the cooked sprouts into the gravy and simmer. Serve the misal in a bowl, garnished with farsan (crunchy snack), chopped onions, coriander, and a wedge of lime. Pair it with lightly toasted pav for a flavorful, hearty meal.
